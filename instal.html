    forget-her-not

    olivia gelard and i collaborated on a gallery show inspired by the essay grandmother spider by rebecca solnit. our show pays homage to forgotten women and our own matrilineal line. we also distributed 4 single page zines.

    2017

    trauma center

    i worked with dani dinstman to create a gallery about processing trauma. using the metaphor of a pediatrician's office and faux serenity to mimic how trauma survivors are often treated. there is a central 'trauma center' focused on expressing said trauma. ps: an article was written about us in squinch magazine. you can read it here.

    2017

    social media quiz

    this installation is a teen-magazine style quiz that plays on the complex unspoken culture of social media. fun fact: i love social media !! don't believe me? check this out.

    2016

    at least my cactus is still alive

    prompted by a six-word autobiography, this installation was an exploration into my experiences with anxiety, depression, and trauma. i wanted to grow from the process of sharing my pain and hope viewers would be compelled to examine their own. fun fact: i cried when i tried to explain the concept for this project to my professor (and half of my class).
