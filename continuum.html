contiuum

continuum is the 2018 bfa thesis show of boston university college of fine arts school of visual arts. i worked with olivia gelard to generate the full brand identity and ad campaign for the show. (we also curated the design half of the show)

2018

#brand, #sticker, #website, #digital, #print

we had a couple of big goals while developing the continuum brand.

1: represent the unity between the graphic design, painting, sculpture, and printmaking majors.

2: pay homage to the 808 gallery where the thesis show was hosted, since it would be undergoing major construction after our graduation.

as design students in the larger school of fine arts, olivia and i always felt the designers were a little separate from the rest of the visual artists (maybe because we were on another floor of the building). but we also believed that designers were just as artistic as any painter or sculptor. so we decided to frame the thesis around the concept of unity between fine art majors.

we chose the name continuum because we believed our class was literally a continuum set of visual artists. mobius strips are naturally infinite and continual, so we decided to use them as our first major visual element.

our favorite part of the 808 gallery was the floor and ceiling patterns. we took simple shapes from them and used them as building blocks for our banner posters and takeaway show cards.

our main shape was the octagon because 8 sides-> 808 building + class of 2018... get it?

we knew we wanted to make stickers because who doesn't like stickers? plus, stickers are a great solution for a guerilla ad campaign in a city like boston. we also wanted to incorporate the stickers into the rest of the brand, so we used them as the geometric forms on the large posters.

finally, we knew there needed to be a web presence for our show. so we bought the url thesis.show (score!!) with the intent that it could exist as an ongoing archive of thesis show participents. it has a digital version of our black and white poster as well as links to the websites or social media of everyone in our class.
