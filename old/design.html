memorywave

this video was made in spring 2017 typography. in it, i appropriate the aesthetics of vaporwave to imitate and examine the way memories are formed and changed. fun fact: my friend made the music used in this video and you can get it for free here.


social media quiz

this installation was made for fall 2016 graphic design. the assignment was using cut vinyl as a media. my concept was a teen-magazine style quiz playing on the complex unspoken culture of social media. fun fact: i love social media !! don't believe me? check this out.


pop up zine

these mini-zines were a week long pop up project in spring 2017 graphic design. we had to create something based on an early design inspiration. i picked the beatles yellow submarine movie because it's always been a feel good movie for me and i was really inspired by the pattern work and aesthetics of the movie. one zine is a collection of work i made in high school inspired by pattern contrasting my contemporary art which opens up to a self portrait in the style of the movie. the other zine is a collection of my favorite lines from the nowhere man.

the meme textbook

designed in spring 2017 graphic design. inspired by my own fascination with contemporary internet culture, this project is a textbook about the chronology, dichotomy, and impact of memes and meme culture. read the full textbook


dump him merchandise

i was commissioned to create a sticker design and a design to be etched on a grinder for the band, dump him in 2016. btw: you should give dump him's music a listen because they are rad.


entropy

this book was designed for an anthology assignment in fall 2016 typography. i chose to curate a series of short stories and poems focussing on the universe's trend towards entropy and the progression from order to chaos. btw: i highly recommend actually reading all of these pieces, especially 'on entropy.'

at least my cactus is alive

this installation was made for my fall 2016 typography class. the goal was to create an autobiography using six words. i attempted to articulate how my mind thinks and the anxieties circulating my life at the moment. the goal was to have a viewer understand how my brain processes trauma. fun fact: i cried when i tried to explain the concept for this project to my professor (and half of my class).
